What We Believe

The next American economy is being built right now.

The question is who will have access to the American Dream for the next generation.

For two and a half centuries, this country has been arguing about who holds power, who benefits, and what we owe one another. That argument has produced extraordinary wealth and distributed it narrowly. Our generation is inheriting the hardest version of the argument yet — about Social Security and healthcare in an aging society, the future of work in an economy driven by AI and automation, and whether ownership and the American Dream are still attainable to ordinary people.

SPCC.1 is an R&D firm for economic democracy. We work with industry partners, founders, and educators to test the guiding principles of the Shared Prosperity Model in real-world settings — in the places where the next economy is actually being built.

Areas We Work In

Industry

Workforce, Shared Ownership, & Community Infrastructure

We work in specific industries on inclusive economic development — designing the workforce pipelines, ownership pathways, and community engagement architecture that determine whether major industrial investment produces broad-based prosperity or extraction.

Entrepreneurship

Founder Transitions & Ecosystem Building

We work with entrepreneurs and founders navigating the inflection points where wealth-building actually happens — particularly the structural transition from founder-centric operations to transferable enterprise value.

Education

Economic & Civic Formation

We work with educators and institutional leaders focused on democratic renewal. We develop curriculum, facilitate cohorts, and co-design new models for economic literacy, civic engagement, and economic democracy — because political agency without economic understanding is fragile.

The Body of Work

The thinking behind the practice.

The American economy is at an inflection point. The post-war model of wages plus redistribution stabilized the middle class for seventy years and is now structurally exhausted. Asset growth outpaces wage growth. Concentrated wealth erodes consumer demand. AI is accelerating the imbalance rather than reversing it. The argument our firm is testing is that broad-based ownership — embedded into the structure of economic activity before extraction, not redistributed after the fact — is the missing architecture. This is the work of predistribution: building the next economy so that prosperity compounds across the people who generate it, rather than concentrating among those who own the platforms that capture it.
